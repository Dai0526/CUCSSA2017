Overview

Eggshell promotes its ideas and motivates the participants for greater ambitions about their startup projects by providing workshops and salons. The key idea and motivation here is that whether workshop and salon will bring true benefits to our participants. If they are just out of the reach of ordinary students, they would be nothing but a display board with unrealistic information. Our goal for workshop and salon is to truly motivate our participating teams to:
1. feel energetic and motivated - find good friends and interact with peers to learn from each other.
2. acquire real information for their potential business, but not boring “talks”.
3, being benefited from our events by delivering positive feedback about our company around others for a better long-term reputation.
Everything we do here is trying to realize these three goals.

Workshop and Salon

The idea of salons is to gather our participants to sit together and talk whatever they want about their startup projects. The main difference between workshop and salon is that workshop is a passive participating experience, in which audiences will listen to other people’s lecture and often feel embarrassed to ask questions. In a salon, however, participating teams sit together and talk about their business plans, ask each other questions and concerns they might have, and gain experience. We believe a question for one startup team has a great chance of being a question for another team, therefore the most efficient way to answer each team’s question is for them to get together and talk to each other whatever they have already known. Through salon, teams are able to communicate with each other, providing an energetic atmosphere and building a better way of peer review and interaction.

Get encouraged to pitch their business ideas to different groups of people. If we didn’t have this salon session, most teams might have never been able to pitch their ideas to other people and gather feedback before the competition. Every salon is a competion demo to practice their pitch.
