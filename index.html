COLUMBIA CHINA PROSPECTS CONFERENCE

Global Conversation Under New Structure

On the basis of inheriting and further developing the essence of previous forums, this year the forum shall continue to discuss developmental strategies and challenges China faces in the current international situation from multiple perspectives, as well as elaborating on the challenges and opportunities China might face in the future. In the previous years, communication between China and other countries around the world has been strengthened. As a result, “Take in, Expand out” is no longer a vision, but a fact frequently practiced by the Chinese government. Though receiving various benefits with this new approach there still remains many challenges. During such times, it is particularly vital to encourage global conversation on deeper levels. The world shall hear China’s voice as it seeks out towards potential common ground gearing for a win-win situation for all parties. At this time we shall cooperate with Columbia University’s Global Centers, Beijing, Columbia University’s Chinese Students and Scholars Association, Columbia Business School and Teachers College at Columbia University to present multiple dialogues between China and the United States, with full support from the Embassy of the People’s Republic of China.

The 2nd Startup Contest

Eggshell-All about Innovation and vitality

Startup Pitch

The challenge that most young entrepreneurs face today is a lack of resources such as partners, VCs and mentors. Eggshell Incubator is a company that not only helps to alleviate the challenge, but also is a company that bases itself in the Chinese community of the greater New York area. In order to achieve the goal of helping young entrepreneurs to gain resources, we plan to hold a competition on April 2nd (tentative), during which VCs and Judges will be invited to determine the best start-up ideas. Those voted the best start-up projects would be assigned to individual VCs for further incubation.

All current enrolled students and fresh graduates from higher educational institutions in United States are eligible to participate in the competition. The initial registration starts on Feb 21, and ends at Mar 15, 2016. All participants/ teams deemed to have great potential will be chosen to enter a series of workshops and salons, which will help them to polish their business plans. After the workshops and salons, those teams would conduct a crowd funding campaign to raise funds and public awareness for the launch of their business. Finally, according to the performance of these crowd funding campaigns, teams will be chosen to enter the Live Pitch, presenting their business plan to a panel of judges and the distinguished members of multiple fields. Finalists will be chosen according to their presentation and Q&A performance. The top team can win a prize of $5,000 as their start-up fund, while the teams coming in second or third place will win $3,000 and $2,000, respectively.
